Wellcome to Swat Most Famous Places

Swat is a stunning valley and a famous tourist destination located in the upper north region of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province of Pakistan. Swat district is situated at a distance of 247 Km (153 Miles) from the capital city of Islamabad at an approximate travel time of a little over 3 hours via the M1 and M16 Swat Motorway.

Shingrai Waterfall

Apart from a number of scenic sites and tourist resorts attracting large crowds throughout the year, there are numerous other stunning places in Swat valley, which are yet to be explored. One of such unexplored places is Shingrai waterfall, at the foothills of Dwasaray peaks about 20 kilometers north of Mingora. Local tourists visit the magical waterfall in multitudes to enjoy its cool and serene environment.

Shingardar Stupa

The stupa of Shingardar is situated about 3km to the northeast of Brikot village, by the left side on the road from Mingawarara to Mardan. The stupa stands in the mouth of a small glen descending from a bare spur above the valley plain.

Shangla Top

Shangla Top is mountain pass which connects Shangla district to Swat Valley. It is the shortest route between the two districts. It has very panoramic views of Himalaya moutain peaks covered with snow, Hindu Kush glaciers and forests full of Pine and cedar trees. Shangla top receives heavy snow during winter times. Alexandar's army passed through this rout in 320 BC.
